T Y P E S O F C O R A L S
Type Abbreviation Description
Soft Soft
The coral has no skeletal structure and a fleshy body. These corals are typically the fastest growing and most adaptable to the ecosystem. Some are viewed as aggressive and invasive.
Large Polyp Stony LPS
The coral has an inner skeletal body and a fleshy exterior similar to soft corals. Theses corals are known for their epic feeding displays and commonly the one of the slowest growers.
Small Polyp Stony SPS
The coral has a calcified skeleton that is covered in a thin flesh which gives it color. Inside the branches live thousands of small polyps that protrude out of hold in the branches.
Non-Photosynthetic NPS
These corals do not have a symbiotic relationship with zooxanthele that allow for photosynthesis. Instead, these corals get their food by filtering the water or catching copeopods. These corals are the hardest to keep due to the constant addition of food deteriorating water quality.
